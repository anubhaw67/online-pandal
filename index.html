Rituals of Durga Puja

Durga Puja is filled with sacred rituals that symbolize devotion and energy. It begins with Bodhon, the invocation of the goddess, followed by Pushpanjali where devotees offer flowers in prayer. This is followed by Kumari Puja which honors the divine feminine, and on the last day, Sindoor Khela which celebrates the goddess’s farewell with color, love, dances and blessings.

Artists & Idol Makers

The magic of Durga Puja comes alive through the hands of talented artisans known as Kumars. Working in places like Kumartuli, where they spend months crafting beautiful idols of Maa Durga using clay from the Holy Ganga. Their dedication and skill make each idol unique, representing the perfect blend of art, faith, and tradition.

Behind the Scenes

Behind the lights and music lies an entire world of teamwork and effort. Carpenters, electricians, painters, and decorators work day and night to create pandals. Various communities come together, blending creativity and devotion to turn simple spaces into magnificent temples of celebration.

Pujo Schedule

Here is a glimpse of what each day of the Durga Puja festival represents:

  • Sasthi: Welcoming the Goddess.
  • Saptami: The day of Navapatrika Snan and commencing of rituals.
  • Ashtami: Sandhya Puja and Pushpanjali along with Dhunuchi Naach.
  • Nabami: Final day of worship along with cultural performances.
  • Dashami: Sindoor Khela and Durga Visarjan.
